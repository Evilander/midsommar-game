# Midsommar: Screenplay & Scene Details Research
## Source Material for Prologue Scene Writing

---

## 1. THE OPENING SEQUENCE (Exact Order)

### Visual/Audio Opening
- **First image:** An intricate folk-art mural/tapestry chronicling the entire plot of the film in rudimentary fashion. Ari Aster wanted this to feel like "a Disney flourish" — beautiful at first glance, horrifying on closer inspection. Inspired by artist Mu Pan (spiritual descendant of Hieronymus Bosch) and traditional 1800s Hälsingland Swedish wall paintings, but tweaked to include scenes of human offerings, ritualistic sex, and bloody sacrifices.
- **The mural depicts (panel by panel):**
  1. A group of people entangled by a tube with a skull overlooking them — foreshadows the carbon monoxide murder-suicide
  2. A woman weeping, comforted by a man, while someone resembling Pelle sits in a tree above watching with glee — reveals Pelle orchestrated bringing the group
  3. The group following Pelle, standing before white-clothed commune members; a figure in a jester hat (Mark) — foreshadows his skinning
  4. A cliff with two winged people tumbling from it — the Ättestupa ceremony
  5. A banquet table, maypole dance, rejoicing figures with skeletons among them — the May Queen coronation masking death
  6. A crowned girl kissing the nose of a bear — Dani and Christian's final fate
- **Audio:** A kulning (Swedish herding call, used to summon cows home but also sung as a farewell) plays hauntingly over shots of a snowy winter landscape, then cuts out jarringly, replaced by the ringing of a telephone.
- **Setting transition:** Midday shot of Hälsingland, Sweden → cold, quiet Minnesota winter night.

### The Parents' House (Minnesota)
- An old couple in bed do not stir while their answering machine records a voicemail from Dani expressing concern about Terri.
- The camera pans over their sleeping bodies. Heavy snow rages outside in a black vacuum.
- A bedside photo shows Dani smiling next to one of her sister.

### Dani's Apartment (Brooklyn)
- **Character introduction (from screenplay):** Dani is described as "a beautiful but delicate brunette" aged 25.
- **Stage direction:** When Dani reads the email, Aster writes "Dani stares at this, anxiety rising."
- **Phone call with friend:** Dani is on the phone with a girlfriend, discussing her relationship insecurities:
  - Friend: "He should be there when you need him."
  - Dani: "But what if I need him too often? If it becomes a chore?"
  - Friend: "Then not the right guy. Because it shouldn't ever be a chore."
  - Dani: "But he never asks for anything from me! Never even seen him cry! So I'm the only one leaning!"
  - Friend: "Or the only one opening up! The only one making yourself vulnerable."
  - Dani: "I'm always leaning on him! Like I even called him today in tears because my sister wrote another stupid scary email."
  - Dani: "What if I'm scaring him off?"
  - Dani: "It's in his tone, like, you can hear it in his voice, he's just working up the nerve to say something."

### Terri's Email
- **The email text:** "I can't anymore - everything's black - mom and dad are coming too - goodbye."
- Terri has bipolar disorder. Dani has been receiving similar alarming messages from her, but this one "seemed different."
- Dani emails Terri back three times with no response, growing increasingly panicked.

### Dani's Phone Call to Christian
- Christian is at a restaurant/bar with friends (Mark, Josh, Pelle) when Dani calls.
- **Key dialogue:**
  - Christian: "She does this every other day, Dani. And only because you let her."
  - Dani: "Well, I don't let her, she's bipolar, so..."
  - Christian: "Yeah, I know. But you do though, babes. You go straight to crisis mode."
  - Dani: "Well, she's my sister and even you said that this email seemed different."
  - Christian: "Yeah, right, but... is it though, really? It's still just another obvious ploy for attention just like every other panic attack she's given you."
- **Stage direction:** In the kitchen during this call, Aster's direction reads simply "FRAUGHT." Then: "She paces when she's not speaking."
- The friend group conversation (which Dani doesn't hear) is Mark urging Christian to break up:
  - Mark: "Dude, you need to stop sitting on the fence with this."
  - Christian: "But what if I end up regretting it a week later, and then I can't get her back?"
  - Mark: "You don't want her back!"
  - Christian: "I might!"
  - Mark: "She needs a therapist, dude. She has a therapist."
  - Mark: "Oh, so then she should call her therapist and not you. It's literally abuse."

### The Discovery
- Authorities discover the family home has been flooded with carbon monoxide.
- **The physical setup:** Two garden hoses taped to two cars' exhaust pipes in the garage. The tubing splits midway:
  - One line routes into the parents' bedroom, delivering toxic fumes to the sleeping couple
  - The second tube was fashioned into a crude mask, strapped/taped directly to Terri's face and into her mouth with duct tape
- **What firefighters/police find:**
  - They gradually climb the stairs with caution
  - Parents' bedroom: the old couple lying dead in bed
  - Terri's room: Her body with the hose/pipe crudely taped over her mouth with duct tape, green vomit (bile) spattered all over her shirt
  - Body bags are zipped; the camera does a steady, smooth pull-in to Terri's face with the mask still attached
  - The green vomit is a deliberate Exorcist Easter egg by Aster — but also medically accurate (bile is bright green, and inhaling carbon monoxide causes vomiting)

### Dani's Reaction
- She calls Christian. He hears her scream and cry "like a wounded animal."
- Christian knows what has happened without Dani uttering a word other than "no" — she repeats this word between screams, and the howl and "no"s morph into a singular sound of sadness.
- "Piercing wails" — Aster "focuses on Pugh's heartbreaking acting while snuffing the sound of her voice." The scene is presented with muted/silenced audio at key moments, making it more devastating.
- **Final image of opening:** Christian sits on the couch "comforting" Dani as she cathartically cries against his lap. But the camera catches Christian's expression: not grief for Dani, but regret and remorse — he realizes he's now trapped in this relationship. Siren lights reflect on windows. Heavy snow falls outside. Opening credits roll over this image.

## 2. CHRISTIAN'S GASLIGHTING PATTERNS

### The Sweden Trip Argument
- Christian: "I told you I wanted to go to Sweden."
- Dani: "No, you said it would be cool to go."
- Christian: "Yeah, and then I got the opportunity, and I decided to do it."
- Dani: "You already have a ticket."
- Dani: "Sweden. I had no idea."
- Christian's tactic: claims he already told Dani about things she clearly didn't know, making her doubt her own memory.

### The Core Pattern
- Christian gaslights Dani into believing she is in the wrong. Frightened by his attitude, she ends up apologizing.
- He claims "I already told you about this, you must have forgotten" — making her the "crazy one."
- He attempts to walk out of arguments (and the relationship) but Dani, terrified by his pulling away, apologizes and admits fault.
- His cursory hugs and empty platitudes make her feel desperately lonely.
- Dani: "You didn't apologize, you said sorry, which sounds more like, 'Too bad.'"
- Dani: "Please, please, please. I'm not trying to attack you."
- Christian: "It really feels like you are [attacking me]."
- Christian: "I really think I should just leave." (said during arguments as a threat)
- Christian: "Maybe I should just go home." (another withdrawal threat)

### The Invitation as Gaslighting
- Christian invites Dani to Sweden — but only because he's too cowardly to break up with her while she's grieving. He pretends his cowardice is "a romantic move."
- To friends: "I invited Dani to come to Sweden. So you guys know."

## 3. DIRECTOR'S CUT ADDITIONS (~24 minutes, 171 min total vs 148 theatrical)

### The Birthday Argument (Major Addition)
- Christian completely forgot Dani's birthday.
- Pelle is the one who remembers — the pathetic birthday cake slice with one candle wasn't even Christian's idea, but Pelle scrambling to help.
- In the extended argument, Christian accuses Dani of trying to guilt-trip him for forgetting.
- He says her kindness (she had given him a small bouquet of flowers) makes him feel "in debt" to her, and claims she does this purposefully.
- He calls her "manipulative and cruel and horrible" because she does nice things for him.
- This seven-minute scene represents the most significant addition to the Director's Cut.

### Extended Car Ride to Hårga
- Additional comedy from Mark talking continuously while Dani yawns.
- Josh is seen reading "The Secret Nazi Language of the Uthark" — explicitly connecting Nordic traditions to white supremacy.
- Pelle tells Dani that Christian "was already brainwashed when I found him" — revealing Pelle's calculated manipulation.
- Dani receives a birthday text during the ride.

### The River Offering Ritual (New 7-Minute Scene)
- Nighttime: two men throw a fir tree decorated with wooden carvings into a lake as an offering to the goddess.
- A young Hårga boy dressed as a Christmas tree volunteers to be drowned as a sacrifice.
- The ritual ends without death — the boy is "saved" by villagers.
- **Critical narrative function:** This scene foreshadows and explains Connie's death. She was eventually drowned in the river in place of the Hårga child.
- Creates ambiguity about whether the child sacrifice was staged or real.

### Christian's Thesis Subplot (Largest Chunk of New Footage)
- Extended sequences show Christian actively researching rituals and interviewing villagers.
- Siv seduces him with promises of unique research access.
- Extended confrontation between Christian and Josh — includes a funny JSTOR joke.
- Paints Christian as even more manipulative — his affair with Maja is explicitly "a passive aggressive maneuver to force the end of the relationship."

### Additional Ritual Details
- Three additional minutes in the welcoming scene, including a new ritual showing Hårga burning meat on an eternal fire symbolizing "harmony and communion."
- Infants inherit names from those who die via Ättestupa.
- Expanded meal scene revealing Mark was also selected as a breeder before defiling the ancestor's tree.
- Expanded dialogue emphasizing the Hårga's need for white outsiders specifically for breeding purposes while maintaining "bloodline purity."

### Connie & Simon's Expanded Role
- British couple invited by Pelle's commune-brother Ingemar.
- They asked Ingemar to officiate their wedding.
- After the Ättestupa, they demand to leave. Connie is told Simon was driven to a train station alone (a lie — he's been killed).
- Connie protests: Simon would never leave her without a message.
- Director's Cut clarifies Connie was drowned (explaining the river ritual connection).
- Simon's fate: lungs pulled out of his body ("blood eagle" style), body displayed later.

## 4. PELLE'S "DO YOU FEEL HELD" CONVERSATION

### Full Dialogue Reconstruction
- **Context:** Pelle finds Dani alone, emotional, after witnessing the Ättestupa.
- Pelle: "I know what you're going through, Dani."
- Pelle: "I lost my parents when I was a little boy. They burned up in a fire. My parents, they burned up in a fire and I became, technically, an orphan."
- Pelle: "So believe me when I tell you that I know what it's like. Because I do, I really, really do."
- Pelle: "Yet my difference is, I never got the chance to feel lost, because I had a family here. Where everyone embraced me. And swept me up. And I was raised by a community that doesn't bicker over what's theirs and what's not theirs."
- Pelle: "But I have always felt held. By a family. A real family. Which everyone deserves. And you deserve."
- Dani: "Pelle, Christian could walk in."
- Pelle: "He's what I'm talking about. He's my good friend and I like him, but... Dani, do you feel held by him? Does he feel like home to you?"

### The Significance
- Pelle is the only character who acknowledges Dani's grief authentically — but he's simultaneously recruiting her for the cult.
- His parents' death "in a fire" is likely connected to the Hårga's practices (they burn sacrifices in the final temple). This may mean his parents were sacrificed.
- The "do you feel held" question is the film's thematic thesis statement: the tension between genuine emotional need and cult manipulation.

## 5. SIV'S PHILOSOPHICAL EXPLANATIONS

### On the Ättestupa / Life Cycle
- Siv: "You must understand it as a great joy for them. And when it is my turn, it will be great joy for me."
- Siv: "We view life like a circle. A re-cycle. One thing falls and another raises."
- Siv: "The first lady who jumped: her name was Ylva. That baby, who is not yet born, will inherit this name and also be Ylva. And if it's a boy, he will take the name of Dan."
- The Hårga justification: Instead of getting old and dying in pain, fear, and shame, they give their life as a gesture before it can spoil. "It does no good dying, lashing back at the inevitable. It corrupts the spirit."

### On the Nine Sacrifices
- Siv: "On this, the day of our deity of reciprocity, we gather to give special thanks to our treasured Sun. As an offering for our Father, we will today surrender nine human lives. As Hårga takes, so Hårga also gives."
- Siv: "Thus, for every newblood sacrificed, we will dedicate one of our own."

### Life Stages (Based on Multiples of Nine)
- The Hårga divide life into four periods:
  - 0-18: Spring (childhood/youth)
  - 18-36: Summer (pilgrimage/education)
  - 36-54: Fall (work/contribution)
  - 54-72: Winter (mentorship/elder)
- At 72, elders perform the Ättestupa.

## 6. THE HÅRGA RITUALS IN DETAIL

### The Ättestupa Ceremony
- **Pre-ceremony:** Morning preparations. Dani is transfixed by Maja singing and people dancing to kulning — the same herding-call song from the film's opening. The woman leading the dance is Ylva, one of the elders who will die.
- **The ritual:** Two village elders sing to each other, are carried up the cliff on litters.
- **Blood offering:** They cut their palms with a knife and smear blood on a large runic tablet/runestone at the cliff's edge. Similar runic tablets are strewn across the peak — generations of Hårgans performing the same rite.
- **The jumps:** The first elder (Ylva, female) lands face-first onto a rock, leaving a "gruesome faceless skull." The second elder (male) breaks his leg on impact but survives.
- **Community response:** The Hårga sympathetically imitate the surviving elder's cries of pain — matching his wails. Then they crush his head with a large wooden mallet/sledgehammer.
- **Outsider reactions:** Horror from Dani, Christian, Josh, Mark, Connie, Simon. The Hårga treat the outsiders' horror as the aberration.

### The Love Spell (Maja → Christian)
- **Shown in a tapestry** before it happens in the film — each step of the mating ritual illustrated in folk art:
  1. Woman cuts pubic hair into a mug (pants off, positioned over the mug with scissors)
  2. Bleeds menstrual blood into the same mug
  3. Pours the mixture into a pie being made
  4. Serves the pie to the intended love interest
- **What Maja does on screen:**
  - Places a carved love rune under Christian's bed
  - Bakes her pubic hair into a meat pie that Christian eats
  - Adds menstrual blood to his food/drink
  - He is given a special drink described as "spring water with special properties" that lowers defenses
  - The combination of rune + ingested bodily fluids + drugged drink = the complete love spell

### The Mating Ceremony
- Christian is drugged, paralyzed, and led into a building where Maja waits.
- Naked female onlookers surround them, breathing in synchronization.
- The ceremony is drug-induced with synchronized group breathing.
- After completion, Maja exclaims she can "feel the baby inside of her."
- Dani witnesses this through a keyhole — triggering her final breakdown.

### The May Queen Competition
- Women of Hårga drink a hallucinogenic yellow tea.
- They follow an elder's dancing instructions around a maypole.
- They dance in circles, then in pairs, then collapse from exhaustion or crash into each other.
- The last woman standing is crowned May Queen.
- Dani wins — her first genuine joy in the entire film. She speaks Swedish while under the drug's influence.
- As May Queen, she blesses crops and livestock, then faces her final choice.

### The Nine Sacrifices (Structure)
**The Rule:** Four outsiders (newbloods) + four Hårga members + one chosen by the May Queen = nine.

**The Four Outsiders:**
1. **Josh** — skull smashed with a sledgehammer for trying to photograph the sacred text (Rubi Radr) after being forbidden
2. **Mark** — face skinned off (the "Skin the Fool" game) after urinating on the ancestral tree. His skin is worn as a suit by a Hårga member.
3. **Simon** — lungs pulled out through his back ("blood eagle" style), body displayed later
4. **Connie** — drowned in the river (clarified in Director's Cut by the child-offering ritual)

**The Four Hårga:**
1-2. **Ylva and the male elder** — Ättestupa (cliff jump)
3-4. **Ingemar and Ulf** — two volunteers who succeeded in bringing outsiders to the community. They allow themselves to be burned alive in the final temple.

**The Ninth (May Queen's Choice):**
- **Christian** — Dani chooses him as the final sacrifice. He is paralyzed, stuffed inside a disemboweled bear carcass (the bear suit), and burned alive inside the triangular yellow temple alongside the other bodies.

### The Final Temple Burning
- A triangular yellow temple is filled with the nine bodies (some already dead, some alive).
- The two Hårga volunteers enter willingly, are visibly calm.
- Christian, in the bear suit, cannot move or speak. The temple is set ablaze.
- The Hårga community watches and wails/chants.
- Dani, crowned in flowers as May Queen, watches. Her face shifts from anguish to what appears to be a smile — the film's final and most debated image.

## 7. ASTER'S WRITING STYLE (Stage Directions for Emotional Beats)

### Minimalism and Precision
- Aster uses single, potent adjectives to convey internal states. Example: "FRAUGHT" — one word to describe Dani's entire emotional state during the kitchen phone call.
- "Dani stares at this, anxiety rising" — simple, visceral.
- "She paces when she's not speaking" — behavior as emotional shorthand.
- Character introductions are spare but specific: "a beautiful but delicate brunette."

### Sound and Silence as Emotional Tools
- When Dani screams after learning of her family's death, Aster describes focusing on the acting while "snuffing the sound of her voice" — the scene is more devastating with muted audio.
- The kulning is used as both opening and recurring motif — a herding call that doubles as a farewell.

### Visual Choreography
- The camera is "constantly moving rather than static" to ease viewers into the horror.
- The pull-in to Terri's body is described as "steady, smooth" — mechanical precision contrasting with the grotesque content.
- Christian's reactions are framed to reveal his selfishness: we see his face processing not Dani's grief but his own entrapment.

### The Fairy Tale Frame
- Aster: "I've always seen Midsommar as a fairy tale." The opening mural establishes this — it's a storybook that contains the entire narrative, visible but unreadable until you know the story.
- The seasonal structure (Winter → Summer) mirrors the emotional arc: frozen grief → blazing catharsis.

## 8. KEY THEMATIC ELEMENTS FOR PROLOGUE SCENES

### The Duality of Every Scene
- Every moment of comfort in the film is simultaneously a moment of manipulation.
- Every act of kindness has an ulterior motive.
- Every ritual that seems beautiful contains violence.
- The player/audience should never be sure which reading is "correct."

### Grief as Vulnerability
- Dani's grief makes her susceptible to both Christian's emotional neglect and the Hårga's love-bombing.
- The film argues that the answer to "do you feel held?" shouldn't require a cult — but Christian's failure creates the vacuum the cult fills.

### The Personal Origin
- Aster wrote Midsommar while processing a painful breakup. He was "putting himself especially into Dani."
- It's designed as a "breakup opera" that "felt as big, consuming, and cataclysmic as breakups tend to feel."
- The horror is domestic and emotional first, ritualistic second.

### Environmental Storytelling
- The murals/tapestries contain the entire plot — visible from the start but only legible in retrospect.
- The Hårga environment is designed to be simultaneously welcoming and imprisoning.
- Light itself becomes threatening — the midnight sun means there is no darkness to hide in, no night to end the day.
